Spain offers extraordinary value for money compared to many Western European destinations. With some smart planning, you can enjoy world-class food, stunning architecture, and beautiful beaches without emptying your wallet.

## Accommodation Savings

Hostels in Spain are generally clean and well-run, with dorm beds costing 15-30 EUR per night in major cities. Private rooms in hostels or budget hotels range from 40-70 EUR. Consider pensiones, small family-run guesthouses that offer basic but comfortable rooms at lower prices than hotels. For longer stays, apartment rentals through local platforms can save significantly, especially when sharing with travel companions.

## Eating on a Budget

The menu del dia is your best friend in Spain. This set lunch menu, offered by most restaurants on weekdays, includes a first course, main course, dessert, bread, and a drink for 10-16 EUR. It is the same quality food that costs double a la carte at dinner. In Granada, Leon, and some Andalusian cities, you receive free tapas with every drink order -- two or three drinks can equal a full meal.

Shop at local markets and supermarkets for breakfast supplies. Bakeries sell bocadillos (sandwiches on baguettes) for 3-5 EUR. Avoid eating on main tourist plazas where prices are inflated. Walk one or two streets away from major attractions for dramatically better value.

## Free Attractions

Many of Spain's top attractions offer free entry at specific times. The Prado Museum in Madrid is free during the last two hours of each day. Barcelona's Gothic Quarter, Park Guell's free areas, and the beach cost nothing. Churches, cathedrals, and many parks are free to enter. Walking tours operate on a tips basis in every major city, offering excellent introductions to neighborhoods and history.

## Transport Savings

Book train tickets with Renfe's advance purchase fares for savings of up to 60%. AVLO and Ouigo offer high-speed train tickets from as little as 7 EUR. FlixBus and ALSA run affordable intercity bus services, often cheaper than trains. Within cities, buy multi-ride transport cards rather than single tickets. In Barcelona, the T-Casual card offers 10 rides for about 11 EUR.

## Timing Your Trip

Visit during shoulder season (March-May or September-October) when accommodation prices drop significantly compared to summer peaks. Midweek stays are often cheaper than weekends. Booking accommodation 2-3 months in advance secures the best rates.

## Money-Saving Regional Picks

**Granada** offers free tapas and affordable accommodation. **Valencia** is cheaper than Madrid or Barcelona with equally impressive food and architecture. **Asturias and Galicia** in northern Spain offer excellent value, with sidra (cider) houses serving generous portions at modest prices. The **Canary Islands** in off-peak months offer warm weather at surprisingly low prices.

## Daily Budget Breakdown

A realistic daily budget for Spain: Budget (40-60 EUR): hostel dorm, menu del dia, free attractions, public transport. Mid-range (80-130 EUR): budget hotel, restaurant meals, some paid attractions. The key is mixing splurges with savings -- enjoy that special dinner, but offset it with a picnic lunch in a beautiful park.
