Spain's conservative **People's Party** (PP) has lost its absolute majority in the Andalucían parliament and will now depend on support from the far-right **Vox party** to govern the southern region, according to [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2026/may/18/spain-conservative-peoples-party-far-right-vox-andalucia-election). The shift marks a significant political realignment in one of Spain's largest and most economically important regions, forcing the traditionally dominant conservatives into an unprecedented coalition arrangement with an increasingly influential populist force.

## The Election Results and Political Shift

The recent Andalucían elections resulted in a fragmented parliament where no single party commands an outright majority. The PP, which has long dominated regional politics, now faces the prospect of governing only with the backing of Vox, a party that has steadily gained influence across Spain in recent years. This dependency represents a dramatic change for the PP, which previously governed with comfortable majorities in the region.

The loss of the PP's majority reflects broader shifts in Spanish electoral politics, where traditional two-party dominance has given way to a more fractured landscape. Vox's growing parliamentary strength has made it an increasingly important player in regional and national politics, even as mainstream parties remain cautious about formal alliances with the far-right movement.

## What This Means for Andalucía's Governance

The PP's reliance on Vox support will likely influence policy decisions across multiple areas of regional governance, from immigration and security to cultural and social policies. Vox has built its political platform around nationalist and conservative positions, and its parliamentary leverage could shape the direction of the Andalucían government on these issues.

This arrangement also reflects the broader challenge facing Spain's political establishment: how to govern effectively when traditional coalitions no longer guarantee majorities. The PP's decision to depend on Vox support, rather than seek alternative coalition partners, suggests a calculation that this arrangement offers the most stable path to governing the region.

## Implications for Spain's Political Landscape

The situation in Andalucía carries implications beyond the region itself. As one of Spain's most populous and economically significant autonomous communities, developments there often signal broader national trends. The PP's need to rely on Vox support demonstrates the far-right party's growing influence in Spanish politics and raises questions about how mainstream conservative parties will navigate their relationship with populist movements.

This political realignment also comes at a time when Spain faces significant economic and social challenges, from inflation and unemployment to regional tensions and immigration debates. How the PP-Vox arrangement addresses these issues will likely influence voter behavior in future elections across Spain.

## What's Next

The formation of a PP-led government with Vox support will require negotiations over policy priorities and ministerial positions. The stability of this arrangement will depend on whether both parties can maintain sufficient agreement on key issues to sustain the government through the full legislative term.

For visitors to Andalucía, the political changes are unlikely to have immediate practical effects on tourism or travel. The region remains a major destination for international visitors drawn to its cultural heritage, beaches, and historic cities. However, the political situation underscores the evolving nature of Spanish politics and the increasing role of far-right movements in European governance.
