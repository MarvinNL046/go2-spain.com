# Germany Expects US Troop Pullout, Eyes on Spain and Italy Next

In a development that underscores shifting dynamics in transatlantic alliances, Germany has described a possible withdrawal of US troops from its territory as "anticipated." According to an NPR report published on May 2, 2026, this move could extend to other European allies, including **Spain** and **Italy**, prompting concerns over NATO's future posture in the region. The announcement comes amid ongoing discussions about US military commitments abroad, though specific timelines or scales remain unclear.

## Background on US Military Presence in Europe

The United States has maintained a significant military footprint in Europe since the end of World War II, primarily to deter threats and support NATO operations. In **Germany**, the largest contingent of US forces is stationed, with bases like Ramstein Air Base serving as key hubs for logistics and air operations. This presence has been a cornerstone of the alliance, especially during the Cold War and more recent conflicts.

The NPR article highlights Germany's stance that such a withdrawal is not unexpected, reflecting broader conversations within the alliance about burden-sharing and strategic priorities. While exact numbers of troops or bases affected are not detailed in the report, it is widely known that the US has around 35,000 service members in Germany alone, making it the most substantial deployment in Europe. For **Spain** and **Italy**, US forces are present at strategic locations such as the Naval Station Rota near Cadiz and the Aviano Air Base in northern Italy, which support naval and air missions in the Mediterranean and beyond.

These deployments have evolved over decades, adapting to global challenges from Soviet-era tensions to counterterrorism efforts post-9/11. Recent geopolitical shifts, including tensions with Russia and changes in US foreign policy, have fueled debates about the sustainability of these commitments. Germany's characterization of the withdrawal as "anticipated" suggests preparations may already be underway, though the report does not specify official statements from US or German government sources beyond this framing.
