# Shakira Acquitted of Tax Fraud; Spain Ordered to Return $70 Million

In a significant legal victory, Colombian singer **Shakira** has been acquitted of tax fraud charges in Spain, according to [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/colombian-singer-shakira-acquitted-tax-fraud-spain-2026-05-18/). The court has additionally ordered the Spanish government to return approximately **$70 million** to the artist, marking the end of a lengthy legal dispute that has drawn international attention to Spain's tax enforcement practices.

The acquittal represents a major reversal for Spanish tax authorities, who had pursued the case against the globally renowned performer. The court's decision to order restitution of the substantial sum underscores the weakness of the prosecution's case and raises questions about how tax investigations are conducted in Spain.

## Background: The Tax Dispute

**Shakira**, known for international hits including "Hips Don't Lie" and "Whenever, Wherever," had faced allegations of failing to pay taxes on income earned during her time in Spain. The case centered on whether the artist owed back taxes during a specific period when she was residing in the country.

The dispute had become a high-profile legal matter, attracting significant media coverage both in Spain and internationally. Tax authorities had claimed substantial unpaid obligations, leading to the formal charges that ultimately resulted in this week's acquittal.

The court's decision to not only clear **Shakira** of wrongdoing but also mandate the return of $70 million suggests that the evidence presented by prosecutors did not meet the legal threshold required for conviction. This outcome may have broader implications for how similar cases are handled by Spanish tax authorities going forward.
