# Two-Year-Old Dies of Heatstroke in Car During Spain's Extreme Heat

A two-year-old girl has died of heatstroke in north-west Spain after being accidentally left in her father's car during an unseasonably hot spell. The child went into cardiac arrest on Wednesday afternoon after spending several hours inside the vehicle in the Galician town of **Brión**, located in La Coruña province. The incident occurred as temperatures across Spain reached 36–38°C (100°F), well above typical May levels.

According to [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2026/may/21/girl-dies-car-extreme-heat-spain), the girl's father had driven his older child to school that morning and intended to drop the toddler at nursery. However, he was distracted by a phone call and went to work instead, leaving the child in the car. The alarm was raised that afternoon when the girl's mother went to pick her up from nursery at 3pm and was told she had not been dropped off. The parents immediately called emergency services, and the girl was taken to a health centre in the nearby town of Bertamiráns, where she was pronounced dead.

Police are investigating the incident, and the family is receiving psychological support. **Brión town council declared two days of official mourning** for the girl, with a minute's silence held in her memory on Friday.

## Exceptional Heat Conditions Across Spain

Spain's state meteorological office, **Aemet**, warned that the country was experiencing "exceptionally high temperatures" for May, with the hot spell expected to last until the middle of the following week. The conditions do not technically meet the criteria for a declared heatwave, but temperatures in some southern regions were forecast to reach 36–38°C.

"Throughout May, we have recorded a prolonged period of below-normal temperatures," Aemet said. "Now comes the complete opposite: a period of very high temperatures for this time of year across most of the country. In fact, some days could break heat records."

This dramatic temperature swing highlights a broader pattern affecting Spain. A 2022 Aemet study found that the arrival of 30°C temperatures across Spain and the Balearic Islands has come, on average, 20–40 days earlier over the previous 71 years. **Rubén del Campo**, an Aemet spokesperson, previously noted that "the summer is eating up the spring," describing the shift as a direct consequence of climate change.

## Spain's Growing Heat Crisis

Spain is one of Europe's countries most exposed to the effects of the climate emergency. The nation has experienced a growing number of heatwaves and a sharp increase in large forest fires in recent years. Spain recorded its highest ever temperature in August 2021, when the mercury in the Andalucían town of **La Rambla**, near Córdoba, reached **47.6°C**.

The incident in Brión underscores the dangers posed by extreme heat, particularly for vulnerable populations such as young children. Heatstroke can develop rapidly in enclosed vehicles, where temperatures can rise dangerously within minutes.

## What This Means for Visitors

Travellers planning trips to Spain during the warmer months should be aware of the country's increasingly extreme heat conditions. If visiting during summer or early autumn, take precautions such as staying hydrated, seeking shade during peak heat hours, and avoiding leaving children or pets in vehicles under any circumstances. Monitor local weather forecasts and heed any heat alerts issued by authorities.
